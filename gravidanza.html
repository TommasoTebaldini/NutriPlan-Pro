📋 Guadagno per Trimestre — Gravidanza Singola (normopeso)

1° Trimestre (0–13 settimane)
Guadagno totale: 0.5 – 2 kg · Fabbisogno aggiuntivo: +0 kcal/die
Focus: folati (prevenzione DTN), iodio, ferro; gestione nausea e vomito
2° Trimestre (14–27 settimane)
Rate: ~0.4 kg/settimana · Fabbisogno aggiuntivo: +340 kcal/die
Focus: proteine (crescita fetale accelerata), DHA, calcio, vitamina D
3° Trimestre (28–42 settimane)
Rate: ~0.4 kg/settimana · Fabbisogno aggiuntivo: +450 kcal/die
Focus: DHA (sviluppo cerebrale), ferro (preparazione parto), vitamina K, colina

📋 Weight Gain by Trimester — Singleton Pregnancy (normal weight)

1st Trimester (0–13 weeks)
Total gain: 0.5 – 2 kg · Additional requirement: +0 kcal/day
Focus: folate (NTD prevention), iodine, iron; nausea and vomiting management
2nd Trimester (14–27 weeks)
Rate: ~0.4 kg/week · Additional requirement: +340 kcal/day
Focus: protein (accelerated foetal growth), DHA, calcium, vitamin D
3rd Trimester (28–42 weeks)
Rate: ~0.4 kg/week · Additional requirement: +450 kcal/day
Focus: DHA (brain development), iron (birth preparation), vitamin K, choline

✅ Alimenti Raccomandati

Proteine di qualità
  • Pesce azzurro cotto 2–3×/sett. (DHA, omega-3)
  • Carni bianche ben cotte (pollo, tacchino, coniglio)
  • Legumi ogni giorno (ferro non-eme, proteine, fibra)
  • Uova cotte (colina per sviluppo cerebrale)
  • Latticini pastorizzati (calcio, vitamina D, B12)
Micronutrienti chiave
  • Verdure a foglia verde (folati, calcio, K, fibra)
  • Frutta secca non salata (magnesio, omega-3, E)
  • Cereali integrali (B1, B6, fibra, ferro)
  • Agrumi e kiwi (vitamina C → ↑ assorbimento ferro)
  • Sale iodato in cucina (iodio)

✅ Recommended Foods

Quality proteins
  • Cooked oily fish 2–3×/week (DHA, omega-3)
  • Well-cooked white meats (chicken, turkey, rabbit)
  • Pulses every day (non-haem iron, protein, fibre)
  • Cooked eggs (choline for brain development)
  • Pasteurised dairy (calcium, vitamin D, B12)
Key micronutrients
  • Dark leafy vegetables (folate, calcium, K, fibre)
  • Unsalted nuts and seeds (magnesium, omega-3, E)
  • Wholegrains (B1, B6, fibre, iron)
  • Citrus fruits and kiwi (vitamin C → ↑ iron absorption)
  • Iodised salt in cooking (iodine)

⚠️ Alimenti da Evitare (Sicurezza Alimentare in Gravidanza)

Vietati — Rischio fetale grave
  • Alcol — qualsiasi quantità (FASD, malformazioni)
  • Formaggi molli non pastorizzati (Brie, Camembert, gorgonzola) — Listeria
  • Carni crude o poco cotte (carpaccio, tartare) — Toxoplasma, Listeria
  • Salumi crudi (prosciutto crudo, salame) — Listeria, Toxoplasma
  • Uova crude (maionese artigianale, tiramisù) — Salmonella
  • Latte non pastorizzato
  • Fegato in grandi quantità — eccesso vitamina A (>3000 μg RAE)
  • Pesce spada, squalo, tonno pinna gialla >1×/mese — mercurio
Limitare
  • Caffeina ≤ 200 mg/die (≈ 2 espresso) — RCOG
  • Tisane non testate in gravidanza (salvia, aloe, grandi dosi camomilla)
  • Liquirizia in grandi quantità (glicirrizina → ipertensione)
  • Integratori vitamina A >3000 μg RAE — teratogena
  • Frutta e verdura non lavata (Toxoplasma da terra)
  • Tonno in scatola ≤ 2 volte/settimana (mercurio)

⚠️ Foods to Avoid (Food Safety in Pregnancy)

Forbidden — serious foetal risk
  • Alcohol — any amount (FASD, malformations)
  • Unpasteurised soft cheeses (Brie, Camembert, gorgonzola) — Listeria
  • Raw or undercooked meat (carpaccio, tartare) — Toxoplasma, Listeria
  • Raw cured meats (Parma ham, salami) — Listeria, Toxoplasma
  • Raw eggs (homemade mayonnaise, tiramisu) — Salmonella
  • Unpasteurised milk
  • Liver in large quantities — excess vitamin A (>3000 μg RAE)
  • Swordfish, shark, yellowfin tuna >1×/month — mercury
Limit
  • Caffeine ≤ 200 mg/day (≈ 2 espressos) — RCOG
  • Herbal teas not tested in pregnancy (sage, aloe, large doses of chamomile)
  • Liquorice in large quantities (glycyrrhizin → hypertension)
  • Vitamin A supplements >3000 μg RAE — teratogenic
  • Unwashed fruit and vegetables (Toxoplasma from soil)
  • Tinned tuna ≤ 2 times/week (mercury)

⚠️ Alimenti e Sostanze da Gestire durante l'Allattamento

Da evitare
🚫 Alcol — passa nel latte (picco 30–60 min dopo). Se assunto, attendere 2h per poppata
🚫 Caffeina — max 200 mg/die; eccesso agitazione nel lattante
🚫 Pesce ad alto mercurio (pesce spada, squalo) — come in gravidanza
🚫 Salvia in grandi dosi — riduce produzione latte (galattofuga)
Galattogoghi (supporto produzione)
✅ Fieno greco — evidenze moderate (3g/die in capsule)
✅ Finocchio — tradizionale, evidenze limitate
✅ Idratazione adeguata — almeno 2–2.5 L/die
✅ Avena — fonte betaosservazione, ritenuta utile
⚠️ Il metoclopramide (domperidone) va prescritto dal medico

⚠️ Foods and Substances to Manage during Breastfeeding

To avoid
🚫 Alcohol — passes into milk (peak 30–60 min after). If consumed, wait 2h before feeding
🚫 Caffeine — max 200 mg/day; excess causes infant irritability
🚫 High-mercury fish (swordfish, shark) — as in pregnancy
🚫 Sage in large doses — reduces milk production (galactofuge)
Galactagogues (production support)
✅ Fenugreek — moderate evidence (3g/day in capsules)
✅ Fennel — traditional, limited evidence
✅ Adequate hydration — at least 2–2.5 L/day
✅ Oats — beta-glucan source, considered useful
⚠️ Metoclopramide (domperidone) must be prescribed by a doctor

❌ Controindicazioni all'Allattamento al Seno

🔴 HIV in Paesi ad alto reddito (salvo valutazione infettivologica specifica)
🔴 T-linfotropo umano tipo I o II (HTLV)
🔴 TBC attiva non trattata (dopo 2 settimane di terapia può riprendere)
🔴 Galattosemia del neonato (deficit enzimatico — latte artificiale privo di galattosio)
🔴 Chemioterapia antineoplastica attiva
🔴 Alcune terapie farmacologiche (verificare in LactMed/e-lactancia.org)
🟡 Ipotiroidismo materno in trattamento — compatibile con la maggior parte dei dosaggi di levotiroxina
🟡 Mastite — allattamento raccomandato (favorisce la risoluzione)

❌ Contraindications to Breastfeeding

🔴 HIV in high-income countries (unless specific infectious disease assessment)
🔴 Human T-lymphotropic virus type I or II (HTLV)
🔴 Active untreated TB (can resume after 2 weeks of therapy)
🔴 Neonatal galactosaemia (enzyme deficiency — galactose-free formula)
🔴 Active antineoplastic chemotherapy
🔴 Certain pharmacological therapies (check LactMed/e-lactancia.org)
🟡 Treated maternal hypothyroidism — compatible with most levothyroxine doses
🟡 Mastitis — breastfeeding recommended (promotes resolution)

🍼 Weaning / Complementary Feeding — Guidelines 2024

Source: ESPGHAN 2017 (2023 update), WHO 2023, SIAIP 2023. Introduction of complementary foods should begin around 6 months (not before 4, not after 7) while maintaining breastfeeding or formula.

🚦 Readiness Signs (all three present)

✅ The infant sits with support and holds their head upright
✅ Has lost the extrusion reflex (does not push food out with the tongue)
✅ Shows interest in adult food

🌿 Approcci allo Svezzamento: Tradizionale vs Baby-Led Weaning (BLW)

📋 Svezzamento Tradizionale (pappe)
✅ Controllo preciso delle quantità
✅ Più semplice per famiglie alle prime armi
✅ Riduce rischio soffocamento
📌 Progressione texture: purea → tritato → pezzetti
🤲 Baby-Led Weaning (BLW)
✅ Autonomia e autoregolazione del sazio
✅ Esplorazione sensoriale dei cibi
⚠️ Richiede supervisione costante
⚠️ Solo dopo i 6 mesi con piena seduta
📌 Cibi morbidi, in bastoncini, senza sale
ESPGHAN 2023: entrambi gli approcci sono validi se garantiscono adeguato apporto di ferro, zinco, vitamina D e assenza di sale/zucchero aggiunto.

🌿 Weaning Approaches: Traditional vs Baby-Led Weaning (BLW)

📋 Traditional Weaning (purées)
✅ Precise quantity control
✅ Easier for first-time families
✅ Reduces choking risk
📌 Texture progression: purée → minced → pieces
🤲 Baby-Led Weaning (BLW)
✅ Autonomy and satiety self-regulation
✅ Sensory exploration of foods
⚠️ Requires constant supervision
⚠️ Only after 6 months with full sitting ability
📌 Soft foods, in sticks, without salt
ESPGHAN 2023: both approaches are valid if they ensure adequate intake of iron, zinc, vitamin D and no added salt/sugar.
